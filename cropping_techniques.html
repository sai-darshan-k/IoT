Monocropping (Monoculture)
(e.g., wheat, rice, maize)
 Marginal, Small Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), microclimate array (temperature, humidity), ESP32, GSM, relay, contactor (Marginal); wall-mounted display (Small)
  • All systems optimized for single crop needs: Soil Probe measures NPK, pH, and moisture every 6 hours, tailored to one crop type (e.g., rice needs pH 5.5–6.5).
  • Microclimate Array: Monitors temperature and humidity (e.g., 70–90% humidity for rice), updating every 2 hours.
  • Relay/Contactor: Automates irrigation based on crop-specific moisture thresholds (e.g., <30% for wheat).
  • Platform: Provides real-time nutrient alerts (e.g., low nitrogen in maize) and pest risks (e.g., stem borers in rice) via SMS.
  • Display (Small): Shows daily soil and weather analytics for uniform crop management.
  • Maximum efficiency for single crop type: 10–20% yield increase by optimizing all inputs specifically for one crop (e.g., precise urea application for wheat).
  • Water Savings: 30–50% via automated drip irrigation (e.g., maize needs 500–800 mm/season).
  • Pest Detection: Early alerts for crop-specific pests (e.g., aphids in pulses), reducing losses by 10–15%.
  • Soil Health: Monitors nutrient depletion from repeated cropping, guiding corrective measures (e.g., add potassium).
Mixed Cropping
(e.g., maize + beans scattered)
 Marginal, Small Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), microclimate array, ESP32, GSM, relay, contactor (Marginal); wall-mounted display (Small)
  • Balances needs of multiple randomly distributed crops: Multiple probes (1 per 0.5 acre) monitor NPK, pH, and moisture for mixed crops, averaging needs (e.g., maize needs nitrogen, beans need phosphorus).
  • Microclimate Array: Tracks temperature and humidity to suit both crops (e.g., 60–70% humidity for beans).
  • ESP32/GSM: Aggregates data for cloud-based analytics, balancing crop requirements.
  • Relay/Contactor: Controls irrigation for mixed root zones (e.g., drip for maize, sprinkler for beans).
  • Platform: Provides generalized nutrient and pest alerts for mixed crops, considering interactions (e.g., beans fix nitrogen).
  • Display (Small): Visualizes combined crop analytics.
  • Natural biological complementarity: 10–20% yield increase by utilizing natural synergies (e.g., reduced fertilizer for maize due to bean nitrogen fixation).
  • Water Savings: 25–40% with tailored irrigation for mixed crops.
  • Pest Detection: Alerts for pests affecting any crop (e.g., pod borers in beans), protecting overall yield.
  • Land Efficiency: Enhances output from small plots (e.g., 1.5x yield from maize + beans vs. maize alone).
Intercropping
(e.g., maize + beans in alternating rows)
 Marginal, Small Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), microclimate array, ESP32, GSM, relay, contactor (Marginal); wall-mounted display (Small)
  • Zone-specific monitoring for organized row patterns: Soil Probes deployed in rows (1 per 0.5 acre), monitor NPK and pH for each crop separately (e.g., high nitrogen for maize, phosphorus for beans).
  • Microclimate Array: Ensures humidity and temperature suit both crops (e.g., maize tolerates 20–30°C, beans prefer cooler).
  • ESP32/GSM: Sends row-specific data to platform for crop-specific analytics.
  • Relay/Contactor: Enables zone-specific irrigation (e.g., drip for maize rows, sprinkler for bean rows).
  • Platform: Analyzes crop interactions and sends tailored nutrient/pest alerts (e.g., maize weevil alerts).
  • Display (Small): Shows row-based analytics (e.g., maize vs. bean nutrient uptake).
  • Precise control of crop interactions: 10–20% yield increase through organized companion planting, allowing precise management of beneficial interactions (e.g., 20% less nitrogen fertilizer for maize rows next to beans).
  • Water Savings: 25–40% with precise irrigation per row.
  • Pest Detection: Reduces losses by 10–15% with crop-specific pest alerts (e.g., bean leaf beetles).
  • Soil Fertility: Enhances nitrogen fixation (e.g., beans improve soil for maize), reducing long-term inputs.
Crop Rotation
(e.g., wheat → pulses → maize)
 Marginal, Small, Medium Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), microclimate array, ESP32, GSM, relay, contactor (Marginal/Small); mini climate station, AI assistance, wall/touch display (Medium)
  • Historical data tracking across seasons: Soil Probe tracks NPK, pH, and moisture across multiple growing seasons, adjusting for each crop in sequence (e.g., pulses restore nitrogen post-wheat).
  • Microclimate Array/Mini Climate Station: Monitors seasonal weather changes (e.g., humidity for pulses, temperature for maize).
  • ESP32/GSM: Stores historical data for crop sequence analysis.
  • Relay/Contactor: Adjusts irrigation for each crop's needs (e.g., flood for wheat, drip for maize).
  • Platform: Uses AI (Medium) to recommend rotation schedules and nutrient plans based on soil history.
  • Display: Tracks soil health trends across rotations (e.g., nitrogen levels post-pulses).
  • Long-term soil health improvement: 15–25% yield increase by breaking pest cycles and building cumulative soil benefits over multiple seasons (e.g., pulses boost subsequent maize yield by 10–15%).
  • Water Savings: 30–50% with crop-specific irrigation (e.g., pulses need less water than wheat).
  • Pest/Disease Control: Reduces pest buildup (e.g., wheat root pests decline after pulses), cutting losses by 15–20%.
  • Soil Health: Maintains fertility by monitoring nutrient trends, preventing depletion.
Sequential Cropping
(e.g., rice → wheat in one year)
 Marginal, Small, Medium Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), microclimate array, ESP32, GSM, relay, contactor (Marginal/Small); mini climate station, AI assistance, wall/touch display (Medium)
  • Rapid transition monitoring between crops: Soil Probe monitors NPK, pH, and moisture for back-to-back crops within a single year, providing fast transition data (e.g., rice needs pH 5.5–6.5, wheat needs 6.0–7.0).
  • Microclimate Array/Mini Climate Station: Tracks seasonal shifts (e.g., high humidity for rice, cooler for wheat).
  • ESP32/GSM: Provides real-time data for rapid crop transitions.
  • Relay/Contactor: Switches irrigation modes (e.g., flood for rice, drip for wheat).
  • Platform: Recommends nutrient adjustments between crops (e.g., potassium post-rice).
  • Display: Shows analytics for each crop cycle (e.g., soil recovery post-rice).
  • Maximized land use through time efficiency: Enables two complete crops per year in the same field, dramatically increasing annual productivity (e.g., rice followed by wheat in same calendar year).
  • Yield Increase: 15–25% by optimizing inputs for each crop (e.g., precise phosphorus for wheat).
  • Water Savings: 30–50% with tailored irrigation (e.g., rice needs 1200–1500 mm, wheat needs 400–600 mm).
  • Pest Detection: Alerts for crop-specific pests (e.g., rice stem borers, wheat aphids), reducing losses by 10–15%.
Relay Cropping
(e.g., wheat + soybeans near wheat maturity)
 Marginal, Small, Medium Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), microclimate array, ESP32, GSM, relay, contactor (Marginal/Small); mini climate station, AI assistance, wall/touch display (Medium)
  • Simultaneous management of crops at different growth stages: Soil Probe monitors NPK, pH, and moisture for overlapping crops at different maturity levels (e.g., mature wheat alongside young soybeans).
  • Microclimate Array/Mini Climate Station: Tracks conditions for both crops (e.g., wheat needs 15–25°C, soybeans need 20–30°C).
  • ESP32/GSM: Sends real-time data for both crops during overlap.
  • Relay/Contactor: Manages irrigation for maturing and new crops (e.g., reduced water for wheat, drip for soybeans).
  • Platform: Uses AI (Medium) to balance nutrient needs and predict pest risks (e.g., soybean pod borers).
  • Display: Shows dual-crop analytics during transition.
  • Eliminates transition time between crops: Maximizes land use by starting second crop before first is harvested, eliminating any fallow period and extending growing season.
  • Yield Increase: 15–25% by optimizing inputs for overlapping crops.
  • Water Savings: 25–40% with precise irrigation during crop overlap.
  • Pest Detection: Reduces losses by 10–15% with alerts for both crops (e.g., wheat rust, soybean beetles).
Alley Cropping
(e.g., crops between mango trees)
 Medium, Large Soil probe (NPK, pH, moisture), mini climate station, ESP32, GSM, AI camera, ultrasonic wind sensor, wall/touch display, solar panel (Large), AI assistance
  • Advanced management of tree-crop interactions: Soil Probe monitors NPK, pH, and moisture for perennial trees and annual crops simultaneously, managing different root depths and canopy interactions.
  • Mini Climate Station: Tracks microclimate under tree canopy (e.g., shade reduces temperature by 2–5°C).
  • AI Camera: Detects pests for both crops and trees (e.g., mango hoppers, crop aphids).
  • Ultrasonic Wind Sensor: Optimizes pesticide spraying under trees.
  • ESP32/GSM: Integrates crop and tree data for cloud analytics.
  • Solar Panel: Powers devices in remote fields.
  • Platform/AI Assistance: Recommends nutrient and irrigation plans for crops and trees.
  • Display: Visualizes crop-tree interactions (e.g., soil moisture under canopy).
  • Multi-year production from both trees and annual crops: Combines short-term income from crops with long-term tree production, creating sustainable agroforestry system with 20–40% higher total yield.
  • Water Savings: 40–60% with drip irrigation for crops and trees.
  • Pest/Disease Control: Reduces losses by 20–30% with AI-driven pest detection.
  • Soil Fertility: Enhances soil health via tree litter and crop rotations, monitored by probes.
